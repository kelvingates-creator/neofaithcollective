*"Forget the former things; do not dwell on the past. See, I am doing a new thing!"* — Isaiah 43:18-19

There's something quietly profound about Isaiah's words. God wasn't telling Israel to erase their history — He was inviting them to stop gripping it so tightly that they couldn't reach for what was ahead. That's exactly the spirit behind a change we made here at NeoFaith Collective.

We moved.

Not physically. Digitally. But for a ministry built on the idea that faith shows up in everyday culture, even a website migration can carry spiritual weight.

For a while, NeoFaith Collective lived on GoDaddy — a familiar, comfortable address. GoDaddy has served millions of websites and there's nothing wrong with it. But as we grew clearer about what this platform is meant to be, we realized our tools needed to grow too. We needed something faster, cleaner, and built for the way we create content — not the way websites were built a decade ago.

So we made the leap to Netlify, and rebuilt the site using Astro — a modern framework that treats content as the star of the show. No bloated WordPress dashboards. No plugin conflicts. No wondering whether the server is having a bad day. Just fast, focused, faith-driven storytelling delivered straight to you.

Was it comfortable? Not entirely. There were moments — staring at configuration files and deployment logs — that felt less like ministry and more like debugging. But that's the thing about new things. They rarely arrive pre-assembled. Isaiah didn't say God would hand Israel a finished road — he said He would *make* one, through the wilderness, through the desert.

We found our road.

NeoFaith Collective now lives at **neofaithcollective.com** — hosted on Netlify's global edge network, meaning the site loads faster no matter where you are in the world. The content management is streamlined. The foundation is solid. And we're positioned to grow in ways the old setup simply wouldn't allow.

This wasn't just a technical decision. It was a faith decision. Choosing to release what was familiar. Choosing to trust that the discomfort of learning new tools was worth the clarity on the other side. Choosing to believe that a ministry committed to meeting culture where it lives should probably live on infrastructure built for where culture is going.

We're not dwelling on the former things.

We're here. We're building. And we're grateful you're on this journey with us.

*Welcome to the new home of NeoFaith Collective. Same spirit. Better foundation.*
